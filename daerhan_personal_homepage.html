About

I am currently pursuing a B.S. in Electronic Information Science & Technology at the School of Electronic Science and Engineering, Nanjing University. My work spans multimodal sensing, computer vision, and machine learning, with a particular interest in how diverse sensor signals can be fused to improve robustness and geometric understanding.

Beyond core research, I also enjoy building complete pipelines: data collection, calibration, model design, evaluation, and deployment-aware analysis.

Selected Research

My recent research explores how multimodal signals such as RGB, active NIR, polarization, LiDAR, and hyperspectral measurements can be combined to build more robust perception systems.

Self-Supervised RGB–Active NIR Fusion for Geometry-Oriented Vision
First-author research
Nov 2025 – Mar 2026
  • Developed a self-supervised single-channel fusion framework for pixel-aligned RGB and active NIR inputs.
  • Designed pixel-wise mixing, stereo reprojection, and exposure-aware reliability gating for geometry-oriented representation learning.
  • Evaluated downstream performance on detection, stereo depth, SfM, SLAM, feature matching, and efficiency.
Per-Pixel Illumination Estimation via Multi-Sensor Fusion
CITE Lab, Nanjing University
Jun 2025 – Oct 2025
  • Built a multi-sensor fusion pipeline combining HR-RGB and LR-HSI for illumination estimation.
  • Reproduced baseline methods and benchmarked performance across multiple down-sampling settings.
Multimodal Information Fusion for Depth Estimation
RGB / NIR / Polarization
Aug 2025 – Oct 2025
  • Developed a multimodal depth estimation framework with custom losses and multi-stage knowledge distillation.
  • Processed and calibrated RGB-NIR data synchronized with LiDAR for densification and mask filtering.
